Delhi: There will be no income tax payable up to Rs 12 lakh - i.e., up to Rs 12.75 lakh including standard 
deductions - under the new regime, Union Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman said Saturday as she read out 
the Union Budget 2025.

In an announcement accompanied by loud cheers and enthusiastic thumping of desks by BJP MPs, led by 
Prime Minister Narendra Modi, she also announced revisions to the tax slabs (again, applicable to the
 new regime only).
                        
Under the revised slabs, tax on income up to Rs 4 lakh is nil.

                Between Rs 4 and Rs 8 lakh the tax will be five per cent.

                Between Rs 8 and Rs 12 lakh it will be 10 per cent.

                Between Rs 12 lakh and Rs 16 lakh it will be 15 per cent.

                Between Rs 16 lakh and Rs 20 lakh it will be 20 per cent.

                Between Rs 20 lakh and Rs 24 lakh it will be 25 per cent.

                Above Rs 24 lakh it will be 30 per cent.
